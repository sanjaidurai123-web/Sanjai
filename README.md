#Netflix Dashboard
Here is a clear, professional structure you can use to document and present any Tableau project:

1. Project Title & Overview
Title: Make it descriptive (e.g., "Netflix Content Strategy: A Tableau Interactive Dashboard").

Overview: A 2-3 sentence summary of what the project is and what it aims to achieve.

2. Business Problem / Objective
The Question: What specific problem were you trying to solve? (e.g., "To understand Netflix's content acquisition strategy and identify the most popular genres and target demographics.")

The Audience: Who is this dashboard for? (e.g., Content Strategy Team, Marketing Executives).

3. Data Collection & Preparation
Data Source: Where did the dataset come from? (e.g., Kaggle, internal company data).

Data Cleaning: Briefly mention the steps you took before bringing the data into Tableau. Did you handle missing values, format dates, or clean the data using Excel, SQL, or Python?

Calculated Fields: Mention any specific custom metrics or calculations you had to create in Tableau to get the insights you needed.

4. Dashboard Design & Interactive Elements
Key Performance Indicators (KPIs): List the main metrics displayed at the top of your dashboard (e.g., Total Titles, Rating Breakdown, Date Added).

Visualizations Used: Briefly justify your chart choices (e.g., "Used a choropleth map to show global content distribution and a horizontal bar chart for top genres for easy readability").

Interactivity: Explain the filters or highlight actions you included (e.g., "Users can filter by 'Type' (Movie/TV Show) or 'Title' to dynamically update the global map and ratings charts").

5. Key Insights & Findings (The "So What?")
This is the most critical section for an aspiring Data Analyst. List 3 to 5 bullet points explaining what the data actually tells you.

Example: "Mature content (TV-MA) dominates the platform, suggesting a strategic shift toward adult audiences rather than family programming."

Example: "There was an exponential surge in content acquisition peaking in 2019, followed by a stabilization."

6. Conclusion & Recommendations
Conclusion: A brief wrap-up of the project.

Business Recommendation: Based on your insights, what should the business do next? (e.g., "Given the high volume of international dramas, Netflix should continue investing in localized content for emerging markets.")
